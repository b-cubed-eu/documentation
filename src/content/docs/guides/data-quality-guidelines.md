This guide provides guidelines for determining the reliability of species status and trend estimates derived from aggregated data cubes from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF). It is based on a comparative analysis between unstructured GBIF data and structured monitoring datasets from Flanders (Belgium) and the Western Cape (South Africa). The aim was to identify the conditions, such as sampling effort, taxonomic resolution, and detection probability, under which models and status estimates derived from these data can be considered robust and suitable for policy-relevant reporting.
